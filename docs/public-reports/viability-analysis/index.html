# PFG — Viability Analysis & Founder Action Plan

Date: February 2026 Scope: Comprehensive project analysis from business, football, crypto, regulatory, and execution perspectives. Includes step-by-step action plan from scratch. For: Founding team / internal use

# PART 1: VIABILITY ANALYSIS

# 1. Uruguayan Football Market Viability

# 1.1 Why Uruguay works

Uruguay is a global anomaly: 3.5 million people producing football talent at the level of world powers.

Key data:

  • 2 World Cups, 15 Copa América titles
  • Exports ~USD 150-250M annually in player transfers
  • Top 5 globally in talent export per capita
  • Recent elite-level players: Valverde (Real Madrid), Araújo (Barcelona), Núñez (Liverpool), Bentancur (Tottenham), De Arrascaeta (Flamengo)
  • Cost of developing a player in Uruguay: USD 30-80k/year vs USD 200-500k/year in Europe

Structural competitive advantage:

  • Operational costs 5-10x lower than Europe
  • Deep-rooted football culture: kids play from age 5
  • Established AUF infrastructure with a clear promotion system
  • Access to CONMEBOL competitions (Sudamericana, Libertadores) as an international showcase
  • FIFA solidarity mechanism: 5% of every future transfer goes to the training club

# 1.2 AUF system structure

Divisional B 2nd 16 ~USD 800k/year Professional, transfer showcase

Realistic path for PFG:

  • Year 1: Entry at Divisional C or D (depending on partnership with existing club)
  • Year 2-3: Promotion to B (requires strong performance + squad investment)
  • Year 3-5: Compete in B, pursue promotion to A
  • Year 5+: Establish in A, qualify for CONMEBOL

Risk: Promotion is not guaranteed. It depends on sporting performance. But the business model (transfers + development) does NOT depend on being in Divisional A. Relevant transfers can be made from B onwards.

# 3. Legal & Regulatory Viability

# 3.1 SAD structure (Uruguay)

Favorable:

  • Uruguay has a legal framework for sports corporations (SAD)
  • The country is crypto-friendly: no prohibitions, regulatory sandbox exists
  • The SAD owns and operates the club; tokens represent indirect economic participation (not shares)
  • Land usufruct is a proven legal mechanism

Risks:

  • The "token <> SAD" relationship must be well-documented legally
  • If AUF has specific requirements on ownership structure, there could be conflicts
  • Token sale terms need review by a lawyer specialized in crypto + sports

# 3.2 Token classification

Current model (Buyback & Burn):

  • NOT a direct dividend — reduces security classification risk
  • Buybacks are a result of operational revenue, not promises
  • Governance is future, not current
  • Clear disclaimer: no guaranteed returns

Howey Test (US):

Prong Assessment Mitigation
Investment of money Yes Inevitable
Common enterprise Yes Inevitable
Expectation of profits Probable Buyback is not a dividend; disclaimers
Efforts of others Yes Holder benefits and governance require action

Verdict: Not a pure security, but in a grey zone. Geo-blocking of US and restricted jurisdictions is ESSENTIAL. A crypto lawyer must review the T&C.

# 3.3 KYC/AML

  • Seed and Private with full KYC — correct
  • Public via launchpad — the launchpad handles KYC
  • Geo-blocking of US, China, etc. — implement with IP check + wallet screening

Verdict: VIABLE with adequate legal counsel. Budget USD 20-40k in legal fees for setup (included in the $300k Club Formation allocation).

# 5.2 Risk #1: The pre-revenue "Dead Zone"

The most real risk is not sporting or regulatory — it's the 2-3 year period where:

  • Holders receive nothing tangible (no revenue = no buybacks)
  • The token has no real utility beyond holder benefits until there is revenue
  • The only narrative is "wait, we're building"

How to mitigate:

  1. Milestone burns: Each major milestone burns tokens — engagement event
  2. Content marketing: Behind the scenes, training videos, progress updates
  3. Real holder benefits: Visits, jerseys, match access
  4. Public dashboard: Let them see construction progress, players, finances
  5. Monthly AMAs: Maintain direct communication
  6. Holder benefits: Tangible benefits from Day 1 to incentivize retention

# PART 2: FOUNDER ACTION PLAN — STEP BY STEP

# PHASE 0: PREPARATION (Now — Before the Seed)

Estimated duration: 4-8 weeks

# Legal & Structure

  • Hire a crypto + sports lawyer in Uruguay

    • You need someone who understands SAD, AUF regulations, and tokenization
    • Budget: USD 5-10k initial retainer (can come out of pocket or from Seed)
    • The lawyer drafts: token T&C, SAD structure, land usufruct agreement

    • Separate entity for token issuance? (consult with lawyer — BVI, Cayman, or Uruguay itself)
    • Legal relationship token <> SAD documented

  • Draft founder agreements

    • SAD equity split
    • Team token vesting (20%, 48 months)
    • Roles and responsibilities
    • Exit clauses and conflict resolution
    • THIS IS CRITICAL. Do it BEFORE raising money.

# Product & Brand

  • Define token name and ticker

    • Must be memorable, short, and available on CoinGecko/CMC
    • Verify web domain availability
    • Register handles on Twitter/X, Telegram, Discord

  • Create visual brand

    • Logo, colors, typography
    • Templates for pitch deck, social media
    • Budget: USD 1-3k freelancer or small agency

  • Build Seed pitch deck

    • 15-20 slides maximum
    • Problem > Opportunity > Solution > Market > Team > Tokenomics > Ask
    • Include land photos, renders, team credentials
    • This deck is your #1 sales tool

  • Landing page / website

    • A simple page with: vision, team, whitepaper link, interest form
    • You don't need an app or dashboard yet
    • Budget: USD 500-1k or do it yourself

# Club Partnership

  • Advance conversations with AUF clubs

    • Define what you need from the partnership: license? divisional slot? name?
    • Have at least 1 LOI (Letter of Intent) before the Seed
    • Seed investors will ask "how will you compete?" — you need a concrete answer

  • Understand AUF registration requirements

    • What documentation does the SAD need?
    • Are there fees? Deadlines? Minimum infrastructure requirements?

# Crypto Prep

  • Choose wallet infrastructure

    • Multisig: Squads Protocol (Solana) is the standard
    • Create the 3-of-5 multisig wallet (initially 3-of-3 with founders, expands post-Seed)

  • Start launchpad search

    • Research: DAO Maker, Seedify, StarLaunch, AcceleRaytor
    • Some require months of preparation and application
    • Apply early, even if launch is 3-6 months away

# PHASE 1: SEED ROUND

Estimated duration: 4-8 weeks Target: USD 850,000 @ $0.05/token

# Seed Preparation

  • Create target investor list

    • Inner circle: friends, family, colleagues who trust you
    • Football contacts: executives, former players, agents, managers
    • Local investors: Uruguayan entrepreneurs interested in football or crypto
    • Most of the Seed will come from people who know you personally

  • Prepare SAFT (Simple Agreement for Future Tokens)

    • Legal document that investors sign
    • Defines: price, quantity, vesting, rights
    • Your lawyer must draft it

  • Define minimum and maximum ticket

    • Suggested: minimum USD 5-10k, maximum USD 100k
    • This gives you 10-85 investors in Seed

  • Setup KYC

    • Sumsub or Synaps: create account, configure flow
    • Cost: ~USD 1-2 per verification
    • All Seed investors go through KYC

# Seed Execution

  • 1-on-1 meetings with investors

    • The Seed is sold in person or via video call
    • This is not a public launch — it's private fundraising
    • Prepare a 20-minute pitch + Q&A
    • Bring data: land photos, renders, credentials, whitepaper

  • Fund reception

    • Define with your lawyer: crypto (USDT) or fiat (bank transfer)?
    • If fiat: bank account of the SAD or issuing entity
    • If crypto: designated multisig wallet
    • Both are possible, depending on the investor

  • Communication during the Seed

    • Weekly updates to committed investors
    • Share progress: "we've raised $X of $850k"
    • Controlled FOMO: "X spots remaining"

# Post-Seed

  • Officially incorporate the SAD

    • With Seed funds, your lawyer executes the incorporation
    • Register with Uruguayan authorities
    • This is a key milestone for Private round investors

  • First tranche release: USD 300k

    • Legal fees, SAD formation, planning, initial team

  • Announce Seed close

    • Public announcement (Twitter, Telegram)
    • "Seed round closed: USD 850k raised with X investors"
    • This builds credibility for the Private round
